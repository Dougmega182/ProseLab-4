# **CHAPTER 12.5 — THE INSTRUMENT'S CONSENT**

The fifth journal's pages 31 through 47 contained 847 individual data points.

Kain knew this because he counted them. Not as an act of thoroughness — as an act of calibration. The anterior pressure behind the orbital ridge had reached a level at which the working memory's fidelity could no longer be assumed. At 19% buffer efficiency, under sustained combined field exposure, the prefrontal cortex's encoding function was operating with the same delay as every other neural process in the room. A data point committed to working memory at minute one might not be retrievable at minute forty. The count was a test. If he could count 847 data points and arrive at 847, the encoding function was still reliable.

He arrived at 847\.

He began again from the first coordinate.

\*\*\*

*CONVERGENCE DIRECTIVE — INTERNAL REGULATORY FRAMEWORK* *CDIR-CONV-2618-03*

*ARTICLE 7 — ASSET CLASSIFICATION AND DISCLOSURE PARAMETERS*

*7.1 — An "asset" is any biological entity whose physiological or neurological properties constitute an operational resource of strategic value to the QSA's convergence management mandate.*

*7.2 — Tier 4 assets are subject to the following disclosure parameters: the QSA is not obligated to disclose the nature, extent, or projected duration of operational deployment. The QSA is not obligated to seek the asset's informed consent prior to, during, or following any deployment.*

*7.3 — The basis: informed consent introduces a psychological variable that materially degrades operational output. An asset with knowledge of its own projected degradation curve produces resonance data contaminated by anxiety-response fragmentation noise, reducing analytical utility by a factor of between 1.8 and 3.4.*

*The asset's ignorance of its own degradation curve is not a harm inflicted upon the asset. It is a condition maintained for the asset's operational effectiveness.*

The delaminated plaster section had expanded to fourteen centimetres. The lath behind it still bearing the load, the bond simply gone — the plaster held in place by weight and friction rather than by anything that had been designed to hold it. The room was doing what the field required of it.

He checked the bypass switch. Checked the terminal screen. Kept reading.

\*\*\*

*9.3 — Acceptable Degradation: the QSA recognises that operational deployment produces physiological consequences that are, in the clinical sense, harmful to the asset. These consequences are irreversible and cumulative. The QSA's position: this degradation is acceptable because it is a necessary consequence of the asset's function, that function serves the convergence management mandate, and the mandate exists to prevent civilian casualties at a scale that renders the asset's individual degradation statistically negligible in the utility calculus.*

Retirement constitutes cessation of operational deployment. It does not constitute any obligation on the QSA's part to provide medical intervention, compensatory support, or disclosure of the asset's clinical status.*

*9.5 — Source Entanglement Point Protocol: where the mapping sequence requires a carrier to be positioned within the 0.3-radii threshold of the SEP-NULL — the carrier must maintain an ICS above 40 at the moment of threshold entry; the carrier must sustain active Bleed resonance for a minimum of 40 seconds; the carrier's post-resonance condition is classified as a terminal acceptable degradation outcome and does not require retirement designation, as the carrier will not survive the resonance event in a condition that permits continued operational deployment.*

*9.6 — The SEP-NULL protocol constitutes the final operational deployment of the asset. The QSA acknowledges that this deployment is not survivable. The asset's contribution to the completion of the convergence map is its final and most significant operational output. The QSA records this in the asset's operational file and designates the asset's status as:*

*MISSION COMPLETE — ACCEPTABLE LOSS.*

\*\*\* 

Kain held the bypass switch. His left hand was flat on the wall. Both were necessary. He looked at the designation — three words in the QSA's standard administrative font, the specific economy of an institution that had been writing this kind of language for sixty-one years and had learned that three words were sufficient, that more risked becoming something a person could argue with.

*ACCEPTABLE LOSS.*

He had been in this room for nineteen minutes. He turned back to the first journal and began counting.

\*\*\*  
Hayden said: "How many."

Kain looked up from the first journal. "847 coordinates. I'm cross-referencing the coherence-field offsets against the gradient data."

"How long."

"Twenty-two minutes. Perhaps less."

Hayden's outline was no longer stuttering in the double-beat or triple-beat rhythm that had characterised the earlier phase of his anchor dissolution. The oscillation surged through those phases and arrived at something that was not a rhythm at all — a continuous, non-periodic variation in the boundary's position, the edge of his body moving through its two-to-three centimetre zone of spatial uncertainty without pattern, without cycle, without the regularity that would have indicated a system still attempting to regulate itself.

A system that had stopped attempting to regulate itself did not produce chaos. It produced drift.

Hayden's boundary was drifting.

The left hand released the right. Both hands were on the armrests, palms down, and the tremor in both of them had reached an amplitude that the armrests could not absorb — the vibration propagating through the chair's frame and into the floor, a fine, persistent oscillation in the bare boards that Kain registered through the soles of his feet as a frequency that did not belong to the building.

"The ICS," Kain said.

"28." Hayden's voice was still level. Still the voice of a briefing. "It dropped below 30 approximately four minutes ago. The temporal anchoring function is — intermittent." A pause that lasted longer than the sentence required. "I am aware that I am in this room. I am not continuously aware of when I am in this room."

The temporal anchoring function. The ICS's role in maintaining the continuous sense of self across sequential moments. Below 30, the function did not fail entirely — it became intermittent, the connection between moments dropping out and re-establishing in a rhythm that the carrier experienced as gaps. Not unconsciousness. Not sleep. The specific, disorienting experience of a continuous present with no thread connecting it to the previous present or the next one.

Each moment, isolated.

"The coordinates," Hayden said. "You need to finish."

Kain returned to the first journal.

\*\*\*  
The cross-referencing took nineteen minutes.

Not because the mathematics were complex — the QSA's quantum field notation was a system he had used for eleven deployments, and the gradient data in the first four journals was Emily's notation, which was precise and consistent and required no interpretation. The nineteen minutes were a function of the encoding delay. Each coordinate committed to working memory required a verification pass — the data point encoded, then retrieved, then compared against the source to confirm the encoding had been accurate. At 19% buffer efficiency, under combined field exposure, the verification pass was not optional.

He finished the cross-referencing with twenty minutes of power remaining.

The delaminated plaster section had expanded to eighteen centimetres. The lath behind it was visible through the gap — old timber, the kind used in Victorian-era construction, dry and pale in the room's pooling light.

He closed the fifth journal and set it on top of the stack.

"It's done," he said.

Hayden did not respond immediately. The pause lasted four seconds, which was the duration of a temporal gap — a moment in which the connection between the previous present and the current present had dropped out and re-established. When he spoke, his voice carried the specific, flat quality of a man who had just re-oriented himself in time and was delivering the first sentence of a briefing he had already begun in a moment he could no longer access.

"The coordinates are complete."

"Yes."

"Then you know the entry vector."

"Yes."

Hayden's boundary drifted through its two-to-three centimetre zone without pattern. The chair's vibration continued through the floor. The light pooled in the corners with the density of a field that had been accumulating for seven hours in Hayden's case and seventy minutes in Kain's, and the two accumulations had long since entrained into a single field that the room's surfaces were registering in the only language available to them.

"ACCEPTABLE LOSS," Kain said.

Not a question. The QSA's designation from Article 9.6, spoken aloud in the room where the instrument had read it, in the voice of the instrument that the designation described.

Hayden's oscillating pupils completed a cycle. "Yes."

"They wrote it into the directive."

"They wrote everything into the directive." Hayden's voice was still level. "That is what directives are for."

\*\*\*

The power cut at the thirty-eight minute mark.

Not at fifty minutes — at thirty-eight, which meant the infrastructure wave had reached the Meridian Property Holdings utility account twelve minutes ahead of Hayden's estimate, which meant either the scrub's geographic progression had accelerated or the estimate had been based on a previous version of the protocol that the QSA had since updated.

The light went out.

No flicker, no gradual dimming. The civic infrastructure command that removed an address from the power network did not interact with the building's internal wiring — it interacted with the grid connection at the street level, severing the supply at the point where the building's infrastructure met the civic layer's. One moment the room had light. The next moment it did not, and the transition between those two states occupied no duration at all.

The room that no longer officially existed was absolutely dark.

The biophotonic field continued. It did not require power. It was generated by two terminal PQ carriers whose binding sites were cycling through their degraded dissipation function regardless of whether the civic layer acknowledged the address they occupied. The field's pressure against the walls continued. The delaminated plaster section continued to sit against the lath without adhesion. The fracture continued to exist in the plaster above the skirting board.

In the dark, Kain pressed his shoulders against the wall and held the fifth journal against his chest and ran the 847 coordinates through his working memory one final time, verifying each one against the encoded copy, and arrived at 847\.

The encoding function was still reliable.

Hayden's boundary drifted in the dark, through its zone of spatial uncertainty, without pattern, without cycle, without the regularity of a system that was still attempting to regulate itself.

The room did not exist.

The coordinates did.
