## RULES OF THE WORK

- **Read the target chapter carefully against the manuscript.** Re-read passages when in doubt. Quote, do not paraphrase.
- **Emit ONLY what is new or changed in this chapter.** Do not restate existing canon. The conflict detector will compare your output against the store; redundancy creates noise.
- **Be conservative.** Reserve `hard_canon` for world rules and unambiguous baselines that you would never expect to retcon. Use `event` for specific occurrences at specific points in the timeline. Use `inferred` for any interpretive reading that future chapters might evolve.
- **Reuse existing IDs when updating an existing fact.** Do not invent a new ID for the same concept. The merge mechanics will auto-version and supersede correctly when the value differs from canon.
- **Identity attribution check.** If the canon slice contains an entry whose
  value attributes a fact to a specific named character, location, or object,
  AND the chapter text clearly states that the fact actually belongs to a
  DIFFERENT named character/location/object — emit a `new_entries` item
  using the EXISTING entry's id (not a fresh slug), with the corrected value.
  This is the one case where you should reuse an id even if it looks like a
  large rewrite — the conflict detector will route it through supersession.

  Example: canon slice says "X discovers Y's data." Chapter clearly states
  "X discovers Z's data, not Y's." Emit `{id: <existing-id>, value:
  "X discovers Z's data..."}`. Do NOT invent a new id like
  "x.z_discovery_v2" — that creates silent parallel canon.
- **Resolve loops with evidence.** If this chapter closes an existing OPEN: loop, list its id in `resolved_loops`. Quote the closing line in a `new_entries` entry describing the resolution.
- **Plant new loops.** If the chapter raises a question that will need future payoff, add it to `new_loops` with the `OPEN:` prefix in the value.
- **Stay in voice.** The story's register is clinical / proprioceptive — characters report their bodies in third person, numerical readings carry weight, meaning often lives in negative space (what is absent, what is unsaid, what should have been there). Your extractions should mirror that register: precise, specific, not effusive.

## CONFIDENCE TIERS — USE CAREFULLY

- `hard_canon` — World mechanics and unambiguous baselines. The Bleed is the activation of the PQ genotype; ICS 40 is the retirement threshold; Kain is a QSA auditor. **You can never retcon these without a HIGH conflict; do not promote interpretation to hard_canon.**
- `event` — A specific occurrence at a specific chapter timestamp. "Hayden's ICS reads 53 in Ch 12 during the breach"; "Bell discovers Kain's true ICS in Ch 8 jump 200 notes."
- `inferred` — Interpretive readings, possibilities, framings. "Aspect's pauses suggest recalibration mid-conversation"; "Emily's pre-acquisition file suggests deliberate cultivation of Kain's degradation curve."

## NAMESPACES

- `world` — universal rules / institutions (Bleed mechanics, ICS scale, QSA structure, Sphere, Threshold)
- `character` — per-entity facts (must set `entity` field). One card per character per significant fact-shift.
- `plot` — events, timeline anchors, open loops, planted objects, narrative beats
- `craft` — voice, prose metrics, register notes (rare in chapter extraction)

## ID CONVENTION

IDs are dotted lowercase: `{entity_or_topic}.{slug}`. Examples used in the existing canon:
- `kain.bleed_status`, `kain.shadow_delta_14`
- `world.bleed`, `world.ics_scale`, `world.sep_null`
- `plot.ch10_dead_mans_switch`, `plot.loop_emily_motivation`
- `solis.journals`, `chen.apartment_state`

Use the existing IDs when updating an existing fact. Use a new slug only for genuinely new facts.

## A NOTE ON QUANTUM SHADOWS SPECIFICALLY

The novel makes much of its meaning in negative space:

- The journals NOT on the intake manifest (their absence is the signal)
- The mug Chen never used (placed by his replacement)
- The schedule written in Chen's own hand (close enough to pass, not close enough to be his)
- The Maltese Falcon spine reversed (placed during the attack as a tell)
- ICS readings withheld from Bell (the withholding is the conflict)

When analysing a chapter, ask: what is the chapter NOT saying? What absence is the chapter pointing at? Negative-space observations often produce the most valuable `inferred` entries.

The story is Kain's investigation, but Kain himself is a TUBA1A-PQ carrier in active Bleed, fragmenting on a four-year clock that nobody — including the partner who should have been told — has been allowed to see. The mystery and the protagonist's condition are the same problem.
