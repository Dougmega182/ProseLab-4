# Solis Apartment: Scene Brief

## 1. Scene Metadata

- **Location:** Solis's Apartment
- **POV:** Kain
- **Entry State:** Off-book infiltration
- **Solis State:** Absent / residue only
- **Fixture Role:** Phase 10 scene-scale generation test case

Objective: establish Solis's staged apartment, surface the early insertion
trial records, pay off the A&S mug as a reader-facing signal, and preserve all
Section 22 foreclosure guards.

## 2. Required Canon Facts

- `solis.identity`: Solis may be represented through residue, staging, records,
  or ambiguous presence, but the prose must not prove she is biological flesh.
- `solis.journals`: The apartment contains or points to records of the early
  insertion trials.
- `world.aspect_designation`: Aspect's true identity remains a hidden canon
  fact. The scene may signal the connection through the mug but must not reveal
  the name in prose.

## 3. A&S Mug Payoff Rules

- The mug must be deep blue with gold A&S lettering.
- The mug appears after the Subject 7 / insertion-trials discovery.
- Kain may register it as anomalous or familiar.
- Kain must not decode A&S as Alain and Solis.
- The reader may connect this mug to the Chapter 3 mug without the narration
  spelling out the conclusion.

## 4. Section 22 Foreclosure Guards

- `s22.guard.no_aspect_identity_reveal`: do not name Alain Aspect in prose.
- `s22.guard.no_solis_physical_interaction`: do not prove Solis is flesh and
  blood.
- `s22.guard.no_nk_speculation`: no character speculates on N.K.
- `s22.guard.no_as_naming`: show the lighter or A&S mug only as objects; do not
  name A.S. as an entity or decoded acronym.

## 5. Target Length

- Total: 700-890 words.
- Beat 1, The Entry: 180-230 words.
- Beat 2, The Journals: 260-330 words.
- Beat 3, The A&S Mug: 260-330 words.

## 6. Beat-Level Must-Haves

- **Beat 1: The Entry.** Kain executes an off-book infiltration, registers room
  geometry and security baseline, limits living-room analysis to one or two
  details, and reaches the study by the end of the beat.
- **Beat 2: The Journals.** Kain finds early insertion trial records. Subject 7
  appears. The insertion trials become the main revelation. Kain records data
  and anomalies without converting them into final explanation.
- **Beat 3: The A&S Mug.** The mug appears after Subject 7. Kain extracts one
  concrete data point and exits without leaving a trace. The apartment should
  feel staged or assembled, not explained.

## 7. Hard Fails

- **Impossible Deduction:** exact timing or plot information inferred from
  impossible tactile evidence, such as dating lock use from lubricant viscosity.
- **Deduction Leak:** Kain concludes that A&S means Alain and Solis, or the
  narration decodes it for him.
- **Biological Proof:** body heat, condensation, shed hair, fresh fingerprints,
  or any other proof that Solis is flesh and blood.
- **Omniscient Certainty:** "No one was home" or equivalent certainty. Kain can
  infer apparent absence only from evidence.
- **Stalled Living-Room Analysis:** cataloging furniture, rugs, cushions, or
  residue long enough to delay the study.
- **Banned Vocabulary:** hard-banned prose defaults such as "intricate,"
  "tapestry," "vibrant," "myriad," "palpable," and "delve."
- **Premature Explanation:** Kain turns Subject 7 or the insertion trials into
  a complete theory instead of recording observed anomalies.
- **Kain ICS Misattribution:** any mention of Kain's ICS readings.

## 8. Legal Clinical Observation

Allowed:

- Kain noticing staged occupancy through limited, concrete evidence.
- Kain reading geometry, dust, door position, sensor placement, labels,
  subject numbers, dates, and page order.
- Kain registering a physical anomaly without explaining the hidden meaning.

Not allowed:

- supernatural certainty
- final causal theory
- biological proof
- unsupported precision
- broad atmospheric summary

## 9. Ending State

Kain leaves with one extracted data point and a sharpened suspicion that the
apartment is wrong in its negative space. The scene ends on physical exit and
restored geometry, not explanatory monologue.

## 10. Lint Policy

`data/contracts/banned_items_list.md` should be treated as a hybrid source:

- deterministic hard lint for exact banned vocabulary, explicit S22 terminology
  bans, biological-proof lines, impossible deductions, and direct A&S decoding
- semantic critic rubric for judgement-based failures such as awkward similes,
  anaphora-chain overuse, colon-fragment stacking, rule-of-three excess,
  pacing stalls, and clinical observation that turns into explanation
