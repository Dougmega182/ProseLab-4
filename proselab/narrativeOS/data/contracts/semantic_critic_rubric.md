# Semantic Critic Rubric

This document defines quality rules that require stylistic judgment. These are assessed dynamically by the Critic and Adversarial Challenger layers, or counted as soft caps.

## Structural Overuse & Stalls (Soft Caps)

- **Anaphora chains (Max 2 sentences):** Do not repeat the same starting word across three or more consecutive sentences.
- **Rule of three (Max 2 per scene):** Avoid lists of three items or clauses (e.g., "A, B, and C") unless used deliberately for environmental listing (e.g., "Three sodium lights. One dead, one cycling, one steady").
- **"As if" similes (Max 2 per scene):** Overuse of comparative "as if" constructions weakens sensory immediacy.
- **"Kind of" / "Sort of" (Max 3 per scene):** Qualifiers that dilute clinical precision.
- **Pulse/heartbeat metaphors (Max 1 per scene):** Avoid cardiac pacing comparisons unless explicitly plot-critical.
- **Underwriting-by-absence (Max 1 per scene):** The technique of narrating what is not felt or present (e.g., "he did not feel...", "it wasn't that he...") must be used sparingly.
- **Colon-into-fragment stacking:** Avoid listing qualities or descriptors after a colon in fragment form (e.g., "He was the perfect spy: quiet, unassuming, deadly").

## Creative Similes & Metaphors

- **Avoid awkward or mechanical analogies:** Banish similes that describe human posture, motion, or emotion in mechanical terms unless the character is literally cybernetic/denervated. For example, "his arm bent at a 90-degree angle, like a pipe..." is forbidden.
- **Sensory Grounding:** All similes and metaphors must be grounded in the scene's immediate physical reality.

## Dialogue & Pacing

- **Dialogue tag discipline:** Default to "said" or omit the tag entirely. Banish overly polished, theatrical, or therapist-like dialogue that explains internal motivations.
- **Stalled Pacing:** Avoid spending multiple paragraphs on spatial analysis before characters interact or move. Maintain narrative progression.

## Atmosphere & Tone

- **Sensory naming:** Scents, sounds, and lights must be directional, located in space, and tied to physical fixtures, rather than general wallpaper.
- **Clinical Observation vs. Final Explanation:** Keep observations objective and proprioceptive. Do not allow clinical descriptions of bodily states to culminate in tidy emotional summaries.
