# Session Log - 2026-06-13 (Phase 16)

## Strategic Intent
Evolve NarrativeOS from a technical causal research tool into an **Artistic Discrimination Platform**. Prove the system can distinguish between 'explanatory power' and 'artistic understanding.'

## Strategic Shifts & Breakthroughs

### 1. The 'Dead Mechanism' Problem
- **Discovery**: A judge can identify technically correct mechanisms while remains blind to the 'art' (e.g., choice, necessity, selection pressure).
- **Pivot**: Implemented the **Mechanism Impostor Test**.

### 2. Choice Attribution
- **Shift**: Moved from "What mechanism exists?" to "Why did the author choose THIS specific implementation?"
- **Impact**: Forces the judge to reason about authorial intent and necessity, not just pattern matching.

### 3. Disagreement Gold
- **Shift**: Refactored the Panel Judge from a voting committee into a **Role-Based Dialectic**.
- **Breakthrough**: Treated disagreement (Detector vs Skeptic) as the primary data signal for identifying 'Dangerous Brilliance.'

### 4. Artistic vs. Synthetic
- **Benchmark**: Seeded the system with 'Synthetic Impostors' (mechanism-perfect imitations) and 'Ugly Genius' (technically flawed but emotionally powerful prose).
- **Goal**: Expose if the system overfits to its own technical rubric.

## Final Status
NarrativeOS now possesses the infrastructure to test for **Artistic Discrimination**. It no longer just praises 'good' prose; it adjudicates the *necessity* of the gears and the *truth* of the implementation.
