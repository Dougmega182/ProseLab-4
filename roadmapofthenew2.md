### Phase 6: The Friction Engine (Algorithmic Prose Suppression)

**Priority: CRITICAL | Status: COMPLETE** Modern models default to smooth, emotionally obvious, median internet prose. This phase built the evaluator agents and deterministic kernel that actively penalize that default state.

- **Heuristics Layer:** Built hard deterministic rules to detect clichés, rhythmic uniformity (via stdDev), and abstract emotion tagging ratios.
- **The Friction Engine:** Evaluator agent that actively hunts for statistically safe tokens and scores predictability, coherence, and interpretive load.
- **Arbitration Kernel:** Pure deterministic math layer that holds veto authority. Forces rewrites if the friction score is too low, ignoring generic structural "perfect" scores.

### Phase 7: Narrative Pressure & Emotional Drift Tracking (Stateful Emotional Physics)

**Priority: VERY HIGH | Status: COMPLETE (Phase 7A)** Moving from "scene generation" to "state transition enforcement." This phase builds the persistent mathematical layers that govern emotional gravity and psychological continuity.

- **Character State Engine:** Deterministic math engine tracking passive decay, trigger amplification, reintegration release, and suppression backpressure (`stateEngine.js`). Tracks canonical attendance vs. probabilistic participation.
- **Bounded Delta Constraints:** Pre-calculates the expected emotional manifold (min, target, max) *before* generation, forcing the generator to hit psychological transitions without rigid exactness.
- **Suppression Leakage & Inertia:** High suppression physically deforms generator constraints (e.g., clipped dialogue). High inertia prevents sudden state reversal from reintegration events.
- **Event Extraction:** The LLM acts purely as a semantic sensor post-generation to extract candidate events with confidence scores (`DIRECT_TRIGGER`, `ANTICIPATORY_LOAD`, etc.) for the deterministic math layer to arbitrate.
